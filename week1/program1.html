Bachelor of Technology (B.Tech)

Overview of B.Tech

Bachelor of Technology (B.Tech) is an undergraduate academic degree program focused on engineering and technology. The program typically spans four years and equips students with theoretical knowledge and practical skills to solve complex engineering problems.

Branches of B.Tech

  • Computer Science and Engineering (CSE)
  • Civil Engineering
  • Electronics and Communication Engineering (ECE)
  • Electrical Engineering (EE)
  • Information Technology

Key Features of B.Tech Program

  1. Duration: 4 years
  2. Mode: Full-time
  3. Eligibility: 10+2 with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics
  4. Focus: Practical and project-based learning
  5. Career Opportunities:
    • Engineering Roles
    • Research Positions
    • Higher Studies
    • Entrepreneurship

Branches of CSE

Software Development
Focuses on the design, development, and maintenance of software applications.
Data Science
Involves the use of algorithms, data analysis, and machine learning to extract insights from data.
Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning
Centers on creating intelligent systems that can learn and make decisions.
Cybersecurity
Deals with protecting systems, networks, and programs from digital attacks.
Computer Networks
Focuses on the design and management of communication systems between computers and devices.
Cloud Computing
Involves delivering computing services like storage, processing, and networking over the internet.
Human-Computer Interaction
Explores how people interact with computers and designs interfaces to improve usability.
Theoretical Computer Science
Studies the mathematical and logical foundations of computing.
